The Descent

The rain fell not in drops, but in sheets of grey static, dissolving the edges of the neon-soaked city. Kael stood at the precipice of the lower levels, the hum of the mag-lev trains vibrating through his synth-leather boots. He pulled his collar up against the damp chill, his eyes fixed on the flickering hologram of a forgotten deity that marked the entrance to the Undercity.

"You sure about this?" Elara's voice crackled through his earpiece, thin and strained with interference. "The Memory Engines down there haven't been calibrated in decades. The hallucinations are... unpredictable."

"I need to know what they buried," Kael muttered, stepping off the edge. The descent was brief, a stomach-churning drop through layered smog, before his grav-chute engaged with a jarring hiss. He landed softly on a rusted gantry, the scent of ozone and stale syn-caf hitting him instantly.

The silence down here was heavier. It wasn't the absence of sound, but the presence of suppressed data. Every shadowed corner felt like an archived regret waiting to be accessed.

He drew his pulse-pistol, the familiar weight offering little comfort against the psychological weight of the sector. Ahead, the rusted double doors of Sector 4 stood slightly ajar. A faint, bioluminescent glow pulsed from within—the telltale sign of an active, rogue mnemonic node.
