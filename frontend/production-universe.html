The Descent

The rain fell not in drops, but in sheets of grey static, dissolving the edges of the neon-soaked city. Kael stood at the precipice of the lower levels, the hum of the mag-lev trains vibrating through his synth-leather boots.

"You sure about this?" Elara's voice crackled through his earpiece, thin and strained with interference. "The Memory Engines down there haven't been calibrated in decades."

The silence down here was heavier. It wasn't the absence of sound, but the presence of suppressed data.

He drew his pulse-pistol, the familiar weight offering little comfort. Ahead, the rusted double doors of Sector 4 stood slightly ajar. A faint, bioluminescent glow pulsed from within.
